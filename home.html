Identify New Zealand animal sign

Get started

1. Compare the shape of your print with the examples

2. Take note of the size of the print

3. Click the reveal icon to find a likely match

Be a wet sand walker: Stay below the high tide mark between August and March to avoid disturbing nesting shorebirds.

The firmness of the ground can change the shape and size of a footprint, the footprint will also change over time.

Please upload your photos to iNaturalist.nz and add them to the Animal footprints in NZ project so we can build a better reference set of images
